# Before/After Comparison

## Adventure Menu

- Before: the first focused button was present, but easy to miss on phone unless the player was already looking for focus chrome.
- After: the focused button carries a stronger ring, clearer contrast, and a slight lift. The menu now feels like it answers keyboard and controller input immediately.

## Pack & Equipment

- Before: the active row blended into neighboring equipment cards and the close button competed visually.
- After: the selected pack row reads as the current target, not just a hovered surface. This reduces hesitation before `Use`, `Equip`, or `Drop` style follow-ups.

## Magic Book

- Before: the chosen spell was only modestly warmer than surrounding cards, and dense controls around it competed for attention.
- After: the active spell reads as committed selection, while non-selected neighbors recede slightly. This makes casting and tray-management decisions easier to scan.

## Spell Tray and Spell Targeting

- Before: the handoff from tray selection into targeting included a deferred `120ms` refresh, which added a small but real softness to the interaction.
- After: tray selection and targeting entry feel immediate. The still screenshots show the same target mode, but the runtime interaction no longer pauses before the handoff completes.

## Reward Choice

- Before: the selected perk was valid but under-emphasized for a high-stakes choice screen.
- After: the active perk has stronger amber emphasis and clearer commitment weight, which better matches the importance of the decision.

## Blocked State

- Before: a tray-full or disabled state looked mostly faded.
- After: disabled actions read as intentionally unavailable rather than visually weak, which improves trust in the rules.

## Settings

- Before: focus on toggles and utility controls was serviceable but soft.
- After: focus is obvious enough to support keyboard and controller-style review without adding more text or instructional overlays.
