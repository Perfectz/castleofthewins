# Comparison

## Title screen

- Before: `New Run`, `Continue`, and `How to Play` were functional but generic.
- After: `Start Run`, `Continue Save`, and `Learn Basics` separate the three entry jobs more clearly.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-01-title.png` versus `after-mobile-01-title.png`

## In-run system menu

- Before: `Run Menu` read heavily as a save manager because the save summaries dominated the layout and the action labels were generic.
- After: `Adventure Menu` introduces the surface as a support hub, the intro sentence explains its purpose, section labels name the panels by job, and the action buttons now read as `Floor Map`, `Mission`, `Character`, `Journal & Log`, `How to Play`, and `Return to Run`.
- Evidence: `before-desktop-04-run-menu.png` versus `after-desktop-04-run-menu.png`

## Journal

- Before: the surface title was simply `Journal`, which did not distinguish it from mission briefing or general help.
- After: `Run Journal` makes it clear that this is the persistent run-history and context surface.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-07-journal.png` versus `after-mobile-07-journal.png`

## Help

- Before: `Help` was accurate but generic.
- After: `How to Play` speaks the player’s question directly, which is better for onboarding and re-entry.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-08-help.png` versus `after-mobile-08-help.png`

## Settings

- Before: `Settings` did not signal that this screen controls device comfort and presentation rather than gameplay rules.
- After: `Device Settings` narrows the expectation and reduces ambiguity.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-09-settings.png` versus `after-mobile-09-settings.png`

## Unchanged control shots

- Creation, town, pack, and magic were captured before and after as control screens.
- These were intentionally left structurally unchanged in this step so the small shipment stayed focused on discovery-backed naming and orientation improvements.
