# Comparison

## Adventure Menu

- Before: the first screenful on phone was dominated by `Right Now`, `Resume Point`, and `Device & Controls`. Useful information, but not the likely reason the player opened the menu.
- After: the first screenful now leads with `Run`, `Saves`, and `System`, and `Return to Run` is the strongest visual action.
- Why this is better: the menu now behaves like a tool before it behaves like a report.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-04-run-menu.png` versus `after-mobile-04-run-menu.png`

## Action priority

- Before: `Save Slot` had the primary button treatment, which made the support menu feel overly save-centric.
- After: `Save Slot` is secondary, while `Return to Run` is primary and full-width inside the system group.
- Why this is better: the most important action in a tactical pause menu is usually getting back to play quickly.
- Evidence: `before-desktop-04-run-menu.png` versus `after-desktop-04-run-menu.png`

## How to Play

- Before: `How to Play` presented two dense text blocks that required line-by-line reading on phone.
- After: the same surface is split into `Core Loop`, `Controls`, and `Dungeon Rules`, with bullet-style chunks that can be scanned quickly.
- Why this is better: hierarchy is now doing the work that paragraphs were previously forcing onto the reader.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-08-help.png` versus `after-mobile-08-help.png`

## Control shot: Run Journal

- Before: `Run Journal` already had a clear title and compact section cards.
- After: it remains structurally stable.
- Why this matters: the step improves hierarchy in the weakest surfaces without disturbing an already-competent support layout.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-07-journal.png` versus `after-mobile-07-journal.png`
