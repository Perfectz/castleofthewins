# Comparison

## Town

- Before: the first-town chip read `Visit Door`, and the primary dock action read `Survey`. Both were technically valid, but neither named the purpose of town clearly.
- After: the chip reads `Services` and the primary dock action also reads `Services`, with the dock note `Open a town door`.
- Confusion reduced: a brand new player no longer has to infer that the labeled doors are the point of town.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-03-town.png` versus `after-mobile-03-town.png`

## Adventure Menu

- Before: the menu opened with an orientation sentence and then a flat grid where mission, saves, map, help, telemetry, and close all carried similar weight.
- After: the intro sentence is gone, actions are grouped into `Run`, `Saves`, and `System`, and `Export Trace` is still present but visually subordinate.
- Confusion reduced: returning and expert players can now recognize the action zone by category instead of re-reading the full button wall.
- Evidence: `before-desktop-05-run-menu.png` versus `after-desktop-05-run-menu.png`

## Control shot: Pack

- Before: pack already functioned as a focused management surface.
- After: pack is visually stable and unchanged in structure.
- Confusion reduced: none directly. This is the proof that the step improved navigation and naming without spilling more text or layout noise into adjacent support surfaces.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-06-pack.png` versus `after-mobile-06-pack.png`

## Control shot: First dungeon HUD

- Before: the dungeon HUD already carried plenty of context through ticker, status, and dock actions.
- After: the surface remains visually stable, which is important because this step deliberately rejected the temptation to add helper rows or persistent coaching text.
- Confusion reduced: indirectly. The UI now depends more on cleaner labels and cleaner menu grouping, not more HUD prose.
- Evidence: `before-mobile-04-dungeon-hud.png` versus `after-mobile-04-dungeon-hud.png`
