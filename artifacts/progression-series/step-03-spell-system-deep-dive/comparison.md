# Comparison

## Before

- Spell Study showed class affinity, tier, school, range or self-cast, and mana cost, but it did not tell the player whether a spell was newly unlocked or skipped earlier.
- Spell cards and tray summaries leaned on flavor-level descriptions such as `Brightens your immediate surroundings and extends sight for a while.`
- `Light` was useful, but it mostly read as a passive sight buff rather than a tactical scouting choice.

## After

- Spell Study now distinguishes unlock timing with `New at Lv X` versus `Available since Lv X`.
- Spell Study, the Magic Book, and the Field Tray now show concrete mechanical readouts such as duration, blast size, or formula-level effect summaries.
- `Light` now combines sustained sight with a local hidden-threat reveal, giving it a stronger, more legible strategic niche.

## Net Result

The spell system is still the same system structurally, but it now explains itself much better and gives one of its weaker sight spells a sharper tactical reason to exist. That is the right scale for a deep-dive step that is supposed to clarify and strengthen the current system rather than replace it.
