# Teaching Notes

## Audit method

- Start with the data table. Spell systems often look balanced or redundant based on cast logic, but acquisition and surface messaging are just as important.
- Check the same spell in three contexts: learn screen, library screen, and fast-cast tray. Many spell systems communicate different amounts of truth depending on where the player reads them.
- Separate "this spell is weak" from "this spell is weakly explained." Those lead to different fixes.

## Reusable lessons

- Utility spells need explicit jobs. If they only read as passive maintenance, players undervalue them.
- Learn screens need progression timing, not just availability. `New now` versus `you skipped this earlier` is a real design signal.
- Shared mechanic text is better than rewriting every screen separately. Once the spell system has one good truth source, the rest of the UI gets better faster.
